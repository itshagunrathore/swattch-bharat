Ways to go Green

If you want to help your planet, do not think that there is nothing that can be done. Everyone plays an important part in keeping the environment clean and safe. This means you can do so much and probably more than you think that you can. In fact, we are sharing with you 40 ways that you can go green. These tips are easy and things that anyone can use, so do not wait!

As you can see there is a ton of ways that you can go green. None of them are difficult, and anyone can use them to their advantage. If you care about the world and want to make a difference, know that you can, as proved with these 40 wonderful tips. You can make a difference in the world. Put these tips to good use and the many benefits that come your way will not disappoint you. It is so easy and so simple, why not go green?

  • Involve yourself in making your city clean and green. Encourage everyone in your neighborhood to join or start a recycling program and buy eco-friendly office products..
  • Keep your tires inflated. Tires that are low of pressure are going to cause more wear on the car and more harm to the environment.
  • If you have items around the home that you no longer need, do not throw them away. Instead give them to someone who would use them, donate to a thrift store or have a garage sale and put some cash in your pocket.
  • Check if you can sign up for green power from your utility company. Also, conduct a quick energy audit of your home and replace electrical appliances with energy efficient appliances.
  1. Turn your computers off at night. Even on energy saving mode you are using a lot of energy.
  2. Turn off lights in the home when they are not being used. This alone can cut energy costs greatly.
  3. Stop rinsing dishes before you put them in the dishwasher. This is a great source of water and energy waste!
  4. Take your own bags with you to the grocery store. You reduce the use of plastic, and we all know how dangerous plastic is to the environment.
  5. Hang dry your clothes rather than use the clothes dryer.
  6. Reduce, Reuse and Recycle. Reduce the usage of packaged food items. Reuse scrap paper. Recycle old jars.
  1. Fix leaky faucets. This includes the toilet and the sinks in the home. Even a small leakage can cause good amount of wastage of water.
  2. Instead of drinking bottled water, install a water cleaning system on your sink. Plastic bottles are usually not recycled.
  3. Cut the shower time in half and see your work into full swing!
  4. Take small shower instead of taking a bath.
  5. Pay all of your bills online. This will not only save you time but will also help to reduce paper wastage.
  6. Unplug all of your electronics when they are not being used.
  7. Pre-heating the oven is often times not necessary and simply eats up energy costs
